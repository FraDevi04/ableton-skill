# Chapter 38: Programming Beats 4: Top, Bottom, Left, Right

## Core Idea
Think of drums in terms of Top (cymbals), Bottom (kick/snare), Left/Right (hand distribution). Jazz = top-down thinking. Rock = bottom-up thinking. Apply physical human restrictions manually.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Top/Bottom/Left/Right**: Divide the drum kit into four zones: Top = cymbals (timekeeping). Bottom = kick/snare (asymmetrical gestures). Left/Right = hand distribution.
- **Top-down vs bottom-up**: Jazz drumming is top-down (cymbals establish time, drums fill in). Rock drumming is bottom-up (kick/snare establish groove, cymbals accent).
- **Physical human restrictions**: Humans have two hands and two feet with limited coordination. Apply these restrictions manually for organic feel.

## Key Concepts
- **Cymbals as timekeeper**: Cymbals (especially hi-hat and ride) establish the time — they're the metronome
- **Kick/snare as groove**: Kick and snare establish the groove — they're the foundation
- **Hand distribution**: Left and right hands have natural limitations — not all combinations are physically possible
- **Physical restrictions**: Humans can't play all four limbs independently — use this to create organic patterns

## Mental Models
- Use the Top/Bottom/Left/Right model when programming drum parts
- Think of drums as a physical instrument with human limitations
- Jazz = top-down, Rock = bottom-up — choose your approach based on genre

## Anti-patterns
- **Impossible patterns**: Programming drum parts that no human could play
- **Ignoring the cymbals**: Cymbals are the timekeeper — don't neglect them
- **Forcing symmetry**: Human drums are inherently asymmetrical — don't force symmetry

## Key Takeaways
1. Think of drums in terms of Top (cymbals), Bottom (kick/snare), Left/Right (hands)
2. Jazz = top-down (cymbals first). Rock = bottom-up (kick/snare first)
3. Apply physical human restrictions manually — humans can't play all four limbs independently
4. Cymbals establish time; kick/snare establish groove
5. Asymmetry is natural — don't force symmetrical drum patterns
