# Chapter 23: Process vs. Product

## Core Idea
Process is scaffolding, not the building. Good process doesn't guarantee good results. Only the final product matters to listeners. Listen critically at every stage.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Product over process**: The final music matters, not the process used to create it. Process is scaffolding — necessary but temporary.
- **Critical listening at every stage**: Evaluate your music critically at each phase, not just at the end. Abandon directions that lead to bad results.
- **Scaffolding metaphor**: Process is the scaffolding around a building — you can admire the scaffolding, but only the building matters.

## Key Concepts
- **Process is temporary**: Once the music exists, the process no longer matters
- **Process doesn't guarantee quality**: You can have a great process and make bad music
- **Listeners don't care about process**: They only hear the final product
- **Critical evaluation**: You must listen critically throughout the process, not just at the end

## Mental Models
- Use product-over-process thinking when you're stuck in process optimization
- Think of process as a means to an end, not the end itself
- Evaluate your music critically at every stage — don't wait until the end

## Anti-patterns
- **Praising the process**: "The way I made this is cool" doesn't matter if the music isn't
- **Ignoring bad directions**: Continuing a bad idea because "the process is working"
- **Process addiction**: Obsessing over workflow instead of making music

## Key Takeaways
1. Process is scaffolding — the building (music) is what matters
2. Good process doesn't guarantee good results
3. Listeners only hear the final product
4. Listen critically at every stage — abandon directions that lead to bad results
5. Don't confuse process satisfaction with product quality
