# Chapter 74: Fail Better

## Core Idea
Each stage of music-making requires practice — including finishing. More unfinished tracks = fewer finishing opportunities. Force yourself to finish even bad tracks. Get practice failing. Return to Scraps and Sketches for material reuse.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Finishing is a skill**: Each stage of music-making requires practice — including finishing. You can't get good at finishing without finishing.
- **More unfinished = fewer finishes**: Every unfinished track is a missed opportunity to practice finishing. More unfinished tracks = fewer finishing opportunities.
- **Force bad finishes**: Force yourself to finish even bad tracks. A finished bad track is more valuable than an unfinished good track.
- **Scraps and Sketches return**: Finished bad tracks become scraps and sketches — reusable material for future tracks.

## Key Concepts
- **Practice finishing**: Finishing is a skill that requires practice — like any other skill
- **Bad finishes count**: A finished bad track is practice. An unfinished good track is nothing.
- **The loop**: Finish bad tracks → extract scraps → use scraps in new tracks → finish new tracks
- **Samuel Beckett**: "Fail better" — from Beckett's "Lost in the Joy of Failing"

## Mental Models
- Use "fail better" thinking when you're tempted to abandon a track
- Think of every finished track as practice, regardless of quality
- The scraps from bad tracks are the seeds of good tracks

## Anti-patterns
- **Abandoning tracks**: Leaving tracks unfinished denies you finishing practice
- **Only finishing good tracks**: You can't predict which tracks will be good — finish everything
- **Deleting bad tracks**: Bad tracks contain valuable material — save them as scraps

## Key Takeaways
1. Each stage of music-making requires practice — including finishing
2. More unfinished tracks = fewer finishing opportunities
3. Force yourself to finish even bad tracks
4. Get practice failing — a finished bad track is more valuable than an unfinished good track
5. Return to Scraps and Sketches for material reuse — bad tracks become good track seeds
