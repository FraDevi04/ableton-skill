# Chapter 25: Breadth Before Depth

## Core Idea
In early/middle stages, work broadly — get ideas out quickly before working deeply on one part. Idea generation is fragile and messy; detail work requires a different mindset.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Breadth before depth**: Sketch broadly first — get ideas out quickly. Then work deeply on promising directions. Idea generation and detail work require different mindsets.
- **Two-phase approach**: Phase 1 = breadth (quick, messy, many ideas). Phase 2 = depth (careful, focused, few ideas). Don't mix the phases.
- **Listening for potential**: During breadth phase, listen for what COULD work, not what already works.

## Key Concepts
- **Fragility of ideas**: Early ideas are fragile — protecting them from premature detail work preserves them
- **Different mindsets**: Idea generation = divergent thinking; detail work = convergent thinking
- **Potential vs. perfection**: During breadth, listen for potential, not perfection
- **Sketching as exploration**: Early sketches are explorations, not finished work

## Mental Models
- Use breadth-first when you need to generate many ideas quickly
- Use depth-first when you need to refine a specific idea
- These are opposite but both valid — choose based on your creative stage

## Anti-patterns
- **Detailing too early**: Polishing a sketch before you know if it's worth polishing
- **Staying in breadth forever**: Never committing to any idea
- **Ignoring potential**: Not recognizing good ideas because they're rough

## Key Takeaways
1. Work broadly first — get ideas out quickly before working deeply
2. Idea generation and detail work require different mindsets
3. During breadth phase, listen for potential, not perfection
4. Don't mix phases — sketch broadly, then refine deeply
5. This approach contradicts "One Part at a Time" (ch09) — both are valid, choose based on situation
