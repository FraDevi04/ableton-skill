# Chapter 6: Arbitrary Constraints

## Core Idea
Deliberately limit your creative options through self-imposed constraints to overcome decision paralysis and unlock new creative directions.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Arbitrary Constraints**: Self-imposed limitations that eliminate valid options to avoid decision paralysis. The constraints are truly arbitrary — you're eliminating good options, not bad ones.
- **Strategic limitation**: "My freedom consists in my moving about within the narrow frame that I have assigned to myself." — Igor Stravinsky
- **Three constraint categories**: Musical (sound sources, expected instruments), Time (deadlines, timeboxing), Space (change venue, limit hardware)

## Key Concepts
- **Decision paralysis**: Too many options = impossible to choose. Constraints reduce options to manageable number.
- **Arbitrary ≠ bad**: The constraints don't need to be "right" — if wrong, remove them during the process
- **Genre as implicit constraint**: Choosing a genre already eliminates vast musical possibilities
- **Freedom through limitation**: More constraints = more freedom from the burden of choice

## Mental Models
- Use arbitrary constraints when facing a blank slate with too many options
- Think of constraints as a "narrow frame" that focuses creative energy
- The fact that constraints are arbitrary means you can abandon them at any point

## Anti-patterns
- **Avoiding only bad options**: That's just good taste, not a constraint
- **Sticking to wrong constraints**: You can and should remove constraints that limit the song
- **Over-constraining**: If you can't work at all, the constraints are too restrictive

## Key Takeaways
1. Apply arbitrary constraints before starting — eliminate valid options to avoid decision paralysis
2. Musical constraints: one sample, no expected instrument, specific tempo range
3. Time constraints: deadlines (self-assigned or external), timeboxing specific tasks
4. Space constraints: change venue, limit what you can bring (coffee shop = no hardware racks)
5. You're likely already using constraints (genre choice, DAW limitations) — make them explicit
