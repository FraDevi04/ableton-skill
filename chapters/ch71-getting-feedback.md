# Chapter 71: Getting Feedback

## Core Idea
Ask for honest criticism, not praise. Best sources: other producers or serious fans in your genre. Internet forums > anonymous sites. Ask for specifics, not "do you like it?" Quick mastering preset helps evaluate unfinished tracks fairly.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Honest criticism**: Ask for honest criticism, not praise. You want to know what's wrong, not what's good.
- **Best sources**: Other producers or serious fans in your genre. They understand the context and can give relevant feedback.
- **Internet forums > anonymous sites**: Structured forums (Discord, Reddit) are better than anonymous feedback sites because reviewers have accountability.
- **Specific questions**: Ask for specifics ("what part dragged?") not "do you like it?"
- **Quick mastering**: Use a quick mastering preset to evaluate unfinished tracks fairly — an unmastered track sounds worse than a mastered one, which skews feedback.

## Key Concepts
- **Praise is useless**: Praise doesn't help you improve — criticism does
- **Source matters**: The quality of feedback depends on the quality of the reviewer
- **Specificity**: Specific questions get specific answers
- **Mastering bias**: Unmastered tracks sound worse — use a quick mastering preset to level the playing field

## Mental Models
- Use feedback when you need an outside perspective on your music
- Think of feedback as data — not all feedback is equal, but all feedback is data
- Ask specific questions — you get specific answers

## Anti-patterns
- **Asking for praise**: "Do you like it?" gets "yes" from everyone
- **Asking anyone**: Not all feedback is valuable — ask people who understand your genre
- **Ignoring all feedback**: Some feedback is valuable — learn to filter

## Key Takeaways
1. Ask for honest criticism, not praise
2. Best sources: other producers or serious fans in your genre
3. Internet forums > anonymous sites
4. Ask for specifics, not "do you like it?"
5. Use a quick mastering preset to evaluate unfinished tracks fairly
