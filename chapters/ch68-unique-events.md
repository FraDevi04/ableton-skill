# Chapter 68: Unique Events

## Core Idea
Insert sounds, gestures, or processing that occur only once. Single events (unusual samples), single musical gestures (one-off pattern variations), single processing gestures (one-time effects activation). Use sparingly.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Unique events**: Sounds, gestures, or processing that occur ONLY ONCE in the entire song. They stand out precisely because they never repeat.
- **Three types**: (1) Single events — unusual samples that appear once. (2) Single musical gestures — one-off pattern variations. (3) Single processing gestures — one-time effects activation (e.g., reverb on one bar, then dry again).
- **Sparingly**: Use unique events sparingly — too many creates predictability (everything becomes a "unique event" and nothing is unique).

## Key Concepts
- **One-time occurrence**: The power of a unique event comes from its singularity
- **Attention capture**: Unique events capture attention precisely because they don't repeat
- **Predictability trap**: Too many unique events makes everything predictable
- **Placement matters**: Where you place a unique event affects its impact

## Mental Models
- Use unique events when you want to capture attention at a specific moment
- Think of them as "surprise and delight" moments
- One unique event per section is usually enough

## Anti-patterns
- **Too many unique events**: Everything becomes unique and nothing is
- **Repeating unique events**: If it repeats, it's not a unique event
- **Predictable placement**: Placing unique events in obvious locations reduces their impact

## Key Takeaways
1. Insert sounds/gestures/processing that occur only once
2. Three types: single events, single musical gestures, single processing gestures
3. Use sparingly — too many creates predictability
4. Placement affects impact — consider where the unique event lands
5. One unique event per section is usually enough
