# Chapter 49: The Rhythm of Lyrics

## Core Idea
Scansion — mark syllables as strong (/) or weak (*). Place strong syllables on strong beats, weak on weak. Emphasize stressed syllables with longer duration, higher pitch, or louder volume. Fighting natural text rhythm = strained vocals.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Scansion**: Mark syllables as strong (/) or weak (*). This is the poetic analysis of rhythm in text.
- **Syllable-beat alignment**: Place strong syllables on strong beats, weak syllables on weak beats. This aligns the natural rhythm of the text with the musical rhythm.
- **Emphasis techniques**: Emphasize stressed syllables with longer duration, higher pitch, or louder volume.

## Key Concepts
- **Natural text rhythm**: Every language has a natural rhythm — English tends to stress the first syllable of words
- **Fighting the text**: Writing music that fights the natural rhythm of the lyrics creates strained, unnatural vocals
- **Multiple emphasis tools**: Duration, pitch, and volume all contribute to syllable emphasis
- **Scansion as analysis tool**: Scansion helps you understand the natural rhythm of your lyrics before setting them to music

## Mental Models
- Use scansion when your vocals feel strained or unnatural
- Think of the lyrics as having their own rhythm that should guide the music
- Align strong syllables with strong beats — this is the foundation of good vocal writing

## Anti-patterns
- **Fighting natural rhythm**: Putting strong syllables on weak beats creates strain
- **Ignoring scansion**: Not analyzing the natural rhythm of your lyrics
- **Over-emphasizing**: Using all three emphasis tools (duration, pitch, volume) on every stressed syllable

## Key Takeaways
1. Scansion: mark syllables as strong (/) or weak (*)
2. Place strong syllables on strong beats, weak on weak
3. Emphasize stressed syllables with longer duration, higher pitch, or louder volume
4. Fighting natural text rhythm = strained vocals
5. Analyze your lyrics' rhythm BEFORE setting them to music
