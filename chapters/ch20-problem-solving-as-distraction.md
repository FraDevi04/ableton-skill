# Chapter 20: Problem-Solving as Distraction

## Core Idea
Technical problems during creative time are "Resistance" — your mind's way of avoiding work. Use what you have. Solve problems during non-productive time, not creative time.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Resistance**: Technical problems that arise during creative time are often not real problems — they're your mind's way of avoiding creative work.
- **Sacred creative time**: Music-making time is sacred. Protect it from "problems" that can wait.
- **Two-time framework**: Separate time into "creative time" (sacred, no problems) and "non-creative time" (for solving problems, learning, organizing).

## Key Concepts
- **Resistance theory**: Your mind will create or magnify problems during creative time to avoid the vulnerability of creation
- **Use what you have**: Don't let missing tools or technical issues stop you — work with what's available
- **Problem-solving is work**: Learning new techniques, fixing bugs, organizing files — all valid work, just not during creative time
- **The temptation**: The hardest part is recognizing a real problem vs. a Resistance problem

## Mental Models
- Use the two-time framework to protect creative sessions from technical problems
- When a problem arises during creative time, ask: "Can this wait?"
- If yes, note it and return to creation. If no, it's a real problem — solve it.

## Anti-patterns
- **Stopping creation for technical issues**: Letting a plugin crash stop a creative flow
- **Misidentifying Resistance**: Sometimes problems ARE real — use judgment
- **Ignoring real problems**: Not all problems are Resistance — use what you have, but don't ignore critical issues

## Key Takeaways
1. Technical problems during creative time are often Resistance — your mind avoiding work
2. Separate time into creative (sacred) and non-creative (problem-solving)
3. Use what you have — don't let missing tools stop creation
4. Note problems that arise during creative time, solve them later
5. Protect creative time fiercely — it's the only time that matters for making music
