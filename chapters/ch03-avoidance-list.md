# Chapter 3: Avoidance List

## Core Idea
When your music sounds too similar to past work, write a list of specific techniques to avoid on the next track — forcing new creative directions and expanding your vocabulary.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Avoidance List**: A pre-planned list of techniques, sounds, or approaches you've decided to avoid in your next track. Analyze your own work for repeated patterns across sound, harmony, melody, rhythm, and form.
- **Negative catalog**: The inverse of Catalog of Attributes — instead of emulating another artist, you're identifying patterns in your own work that should be avoided.

## Key Concepts
- **Style consistency trap**: Having a personal style is good, but being "bound" by it prevents new creation
- **Revealed similarity**: You often notice your own patterns only after distance — listening back days later
- **Alternative generation**: For each avoided technique, brainstorm alternatives — this reveals WHY you relied on the technique
- **Genre considerations**: In commercial styles (EDM), predictability IS the style; radical deviation may not make sense

## Mental Models
- Use the avoidance list when you detect your music becoming formulaic
- Think of it as a creative constraint that forces vocabulary expansion
- The alternatives you generate for each avoided technique often reveal deeper creative needs

## Anti-patterns
- **Avoiding everything**: Don't eliminate so much that you can't work — be selective
- **Rigid adherence**: You should feel free to abandon restrictions if they'd make the song worse
- **Ignoring the root cause**: Avoiding a technique without understanding why you used it misses the deeper issue

## Key Takeaways
1. Analyze your recent work for repeated patterns across all musical categories
2. Write specific, actionable items to avoid — not vague goals
3. Generate alternatives for each avoided technique — this is where growth happens
4. The avoidance list may feel restrictive at first, but it expands your vocabulary
5. Deviate from the list if the song truly needs the "forbidden" technique
