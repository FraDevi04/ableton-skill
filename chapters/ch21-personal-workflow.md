# Chapter 21: Personal Workflow

## Core Idea
There is no objective right answer for workflow. Try different methods, keep what works, synthesize your own. Practice until it becomes flow.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Workflow as personal synthesis**: No objective right answer. Try different methods, keep what works, discard what doesn't, synthesize your own.
- **Workflow questions**: What to do? In what order? When to move on? When is it done? These are the core workflow decisions.
- **Practice creates flow**: Workflow becomes automatic through practice — it's a skill, not a fixed system.

## Key Concepts
- **Workflow is personal**: What works for one producer may not work for another
- **Workflow is dynamic**: Your workflow should evolve as you evolve
- **Flow state**: The goal of workflow practice is to make decisions automatic, freeing creative energy
- **Workflow vs. process**: Workflow = how you work; process = the methodology you follow

## Mental Models
- Use personal workflow thinking when your current process feels inefficient
- Think of workflow as a skill to develop, not a system to find
- Practice your workflow deliberately — it becomes automatic with repetition

## Anti-patterns
- **Copying someone else's workflow**: What works for them may not work for you
- **Sticking with what doesn't work**: If a method frustrates you, change it
- **Perpetual tweaking**: Don't spend more time optimizing workflow than making music

## Key Takeaways
1. There is no objective right answer for workflow — synthesize your own
2. Try different methods, keep what works, discard what doesn't
3. Core workflow questions: What to do? Order? When to move on? When done?
4. Workflow becomes automatic through practice — it's a skill
5. Don't spend more time optimizing workflow than making music
