# Chapter 7: "Write Drunk; Edit Sober"

## Core Idea
Compartmentalize creative work into two discrete phases: creation (capture everything, judge nothing) and editing (ruthlessly curate, delete nothing-new). Never mix the two modes.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Creation Phase**: Goal = capture maximum material. Only move forward in time. Delete nothing. Judge nothing. Aim for speed and quantity. Be prepared to make mistakes.
- **Editing Phase**: Goal = refine material from creation phase. No new material generation. Ruthlessly delete, trim, reorder, curate. Expect to throw away the majority of creation output.
- **The metaphor**: "Write drunk; edit sober" — drunkenness = uninhibited creation, sobriety = disciplined editing. Both necessary, neither without the other.

## Key Concepts
- **Flow disruption**: Stopping mid-creation to edit/correct breaks creative flow and makes it hard to return
- **Judgment is the enemy of creation**: Creating and judging use different brain modes
- **Most creation output is terrible**: This is healthy — you can't curate without raw material
- **Scraps folder**: Save amazing-but-wrong material for future tracks

## Mental Models
- Use two-phase workflow for all creative projects
- Treat creation as "jamming" — record everything, follow instincts, forget you're recording
- Treat editing as "curation" — listen through once, decide what stays, what goes, what transforms
- The balance between play (creation) and work (editing) generates better results than either alone

## Anti-patterns
- **Editing while creating**: Stops flow, creates self-doubt, slows output
- **Creating without editing**: Endless raw material, nothing finished
- **Judging creation output**: "This is bad" kills the creative impulse

## Key Takeaways
1. Force two discrete phases: creation (capture everything) and editing (curate only)
2. During creation: always recording, no deleting, no judging, follow instinct
3. During editing: no new material, ruthless curation, expect to throw away most of creation
4. Save scrap material that's great but wrong for the current song
5. Use different DAWs or environments to physically separate creation from editing
