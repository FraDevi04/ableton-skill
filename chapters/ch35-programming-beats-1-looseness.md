# Chapter 35: Programming Beats 1: On Looseness

## Core Idea
Apply quantization at partial amounts, quantize only certain instruments, find your natural tempo, and manually shift elements behind or ahead of the beat for human feel.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Partial quantization**: Apply quantization at partial amounts (e.g., 50% quantization). Not all-or-nothing — find the sweet spot between rigid and messy.
- **Selective quantization**: Quantize only certain instruments (e.g., kick and snare quantized, hi-hats loose). Different instruments have different "human" needs.
- **Natural tempo**: Find the tempo at which you naturally tap your foot or play. Programming at your natural tempo creates more human-feeling beats.
- **Behind/ahead of the beat**: Shift elements behind the beat (laid-back, relaxed) or ahead of the beat (driving, urgent).

## Key Concepts
- **Quantization is a spectrum**: Not just "quantized" or "not quantized" — there are many degrees between
- **Different instruments, different needs**: Kick drums might need tighter quantization than hi-hats
- **Velocity matters**: Human beats have velocity variation — not all hits are equally loud
- **Humanize after quantization**: Quantize first, then humanize — not the other way around

## Mental Models
- Use partial quantization when fully quantized beats feel too rigid
- Think of quantization as a dial, not a switch
- Behind the beat = relaxed; ahead of the beat = urgent

## Anti-patterns
- **Full quantization**: Quantizing everything to grid creates robotic beats
- **No quantization**: Not quantizing anything creates inconsistent beats
- **Ignoring velocity**: Human beats have dynamic variation — static velocity sounds artificial

## Key Takeaways
1. Apply quantization at partial amounts — find the sweet spot
2. Quantize only certain instruments — different instruments have different needs
3. Find your natural tempo — programming at your natural tempo creates human-feeling beats
4. Manually shift elements behind (laid-back) or ahead (driving) of the beat
5. Adjust velocity for dynamic variation — humanize after quantization
