# Chapter 5: Mise en Place

## Core Idea
Prepare your creative environment, ingredients, and yourself BEFORE starting a session so that when inspiration strikes, nothing interrupts the flow.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Mise en Place** (French: "put in place"): From culinary world — prepare tools and ingredients before cooking. Applied to music: set up everything needed before the creative impulse.
- **Three preparation layers**: Ingredients (DAWs, samples, hardware), Workspace (comfort, organization), Yourself (hunger, distractions, responsibilities).
- **Template strategy**: Save empty template projects with your常用 instruments loaded for instant starts.
- **Organize as you go**: Name tracks, use colors, delete what you've rejected — maintain order during the creative heat.

## Key Concepts
- **Creative impulse is fleeting**: Once lost, huge amounts of time pass before it returns
- **Non-creative problems kill flow**: Equipment issues, missing samples, uncomfortable chair
- **Preparation time ≠ creative time**: Do mise en place when NOT inspired, not during inspiration
- **Always be ready to capture**: Field recorder, phone app, notebook — ideas come outside the studio

## Mental Models
- Use mise en place as a pre-flight checklist before any creative session
- Think of your studio setup like a chef's kitchen — everything within reach
- Organizing is a creative-killer if done during inspiration; do it during preparation time

## Anti-patterns
- **Getting organized while inspired**: Prioritize music-making over organization during creative moments
- **Ignoring the self**: Hunger, thirst, pets, responsibilities — all kill flow
- **No templates**: Rebuilding your environment from scratch wastes precious creative time

## Key Takeaways
1. Prepare ingredients, workspace, and yourself BEFORE creative time
2. Make templates for different scenarios (solo, collaboration, genre-specific)
3. Organize as you go during sessions — name tracks, use colors, delete rejected material
4. Always be ready to capture ideas outside the studio
5. Never start getting organized when you're actually inspired — that time is sacred
