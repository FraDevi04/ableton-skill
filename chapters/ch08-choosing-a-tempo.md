# Chapter 8: Choosing a Tempo

## Core Idea
Tempo choice is both genre-defined and deeply personal. Use genre ranges as a starting point, then experiment to find the tempo that makes YOUR song work best.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Genre tempo ranges**: Dub 60-90, Hip-hop 60-100, Downtempo 90-120, House 115-130, Techno/Trance 120-140, Dubstep 135-145, Drum & Bass 160-180. These are guidelines, not rules.
- **Half-time/double-time dichotomy**: In fast genres (dubstep, DnB), music at half speed can layer with music at "actual" tempo. A 170 bpm DnB track works with a breakbeat at 85 bpm.
- **Context testing**: Try changing tempo while working — one may feel "right" even if the difference is subtle (124 vs 125 bpm).

## Key Concepts
- **Tempo as genre identifier**: In many electronic genres, tempo is a defining characteristic
- **Personal preference**: The correct tempo is the one that makes the song work — only you can decide
- **Subtle differences matter**: 124 vs 125 may be decisive in some contexts
- **Continuous tempo shift**: Some recent dubstep/DnB creates fluid shifts between half-time and double-time

## Mental Models
- Start with the genre range, then experiment within and slightly outside it
- Test tempo in different monitoring contexts (different room, different volume)
- Don't overthink — if you can't tell the difference, pick one and move on

## Anti-patterns
- **Genre rigidity**: Using genre ranges as absolute rules rather than starting points
- **Ignoring context**: Not testing how tempo feels in unusual monitoring situations
- **Endless tempo tweaking**: Spending more time on tempo than the music itself

## Key Takeaways
1. Genre ranges are starting points, not rules — exceptions abound
2. Start in the middle of the range, then experiment with changes
3. Test tempo in different listening contexts (different room, headphones off, etc.)
4. Half-time/double-time layering is a powerful creative tool in bass music
5. The correct tempo is the one that makes the song work — a creative decision only you can make
