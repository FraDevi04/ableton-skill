# Chapter 19: Scraps and Sketches

## Core Idea
Treat unfinished projects as a library of musical ideas, not failures. Extract elements for reuse in new tracks — even a single 2-bar loop might become your next great song.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Scraps and Sketches**: Unfinished projects are NOT failures — they're a library of potentially reusable material. Extract elements (loops, melodies, drum patterns, sound designs) for future tracks.
- **Sketch library**: Save even a single 2-bar loop. You might not know what you need until you need it.
- **Recovery process**: When starting a new track, review your scraps first — you might find exactly what you need.

## Key Concepts
- **Failure reframing**: Unfinished projects are data, not waste
- **Library thinking**: Your scraps are a resource, not a graveyard
- **Unexpected utility**: A scrap from one genre might be perfect for another
- **The sunk cost fallacy**: Don't keep finishing a bad track just because you've invested time

## Mental Models
- Use scraps as a starting point when you feel stuck
- Think of your unfinished projects as a personal sample library
- Review scraps before starting from scratch — you might find gold

## Anti-patterns
- **Deleting unfinished projects**: Destroying potential material
- **Hoarding without organizing**: Saving scraps without a system to find them
- **Never revisiting scraps**: Saving material but never looking at it again

## Key Takeaways
1. Unfinished projects are NOT failures — they're a library of material
2. Extract elements (loops, melodies, sounds) for reuse in new tracks
3. Save even tiny fragments — a 2-bar loop might be perfect later
4. Review your scraps before starting from scratch
5. Don't feel obligated to finish tracks just because you started them
