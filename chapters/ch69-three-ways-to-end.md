# Chapter 69: Three Ways to End

## Core Idea
(1) Mirror what you did at the start — reverse the accumulation. (2) Loop and add/subtract layers at each repeat. (3) Fade out — only if closing material would work looping indefinitely.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Three ending strategies**:
  1. **Mirror the start**: Reverse the accumulation process. If you built up from nothing, take away what you added.
  2. **Loop and modify**: Loop the closing material and add/subtract layers at each repeat. Each repeat is slightly different.
  3. **Fade out**: Only use fade-out if the closing material would work looping indefinitely. A fade-out is essentially an infinite loop with decreasing volume.

## Key Concepts
- **Mirroring**: The most satisfying endings mirror the beginning — if you started with nothing and built up, end by taking away
- **Loop modification**: Looping with variation creates interest without changing the closing material
- **Fade-out conditions**: A fade-out only works if the closing material is good enough to loop — if it's boring, fading it out just prolongs the boredom
- **Intentional endings**: Choose your ending strategy deliberately — don't just stop or fade

## Mental Models
- Use the three strategies when you need to end a track
- Think of the ending as the final statement — it should be deliberate
- Mirroring creates symmetry; looping creates interest; fading creates atmosphere

## Anti-patterns
- **Random stopping**: Just stopping the track is an anti-ending
- **Fading bad material**: Fading out boring material just prolongs boredom
- **No ending strategy**: Not choosing a deliberate ending strategy

## Key Takeaways
1. Three ways to end: (1) Mirror the start, (2) Loop and modify, (3) Fade out
2. Mirroring: reverse the accumulation process
3. Loop and modify: loop closing material, add/subtract layers each repeat
4. Fade out: only if closing material would work looping indefinitely
5. Choose your ending deliberately — it's the final statement
