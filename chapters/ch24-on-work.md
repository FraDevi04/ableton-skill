# Chapter 24: On Work

## Core Idea
There are no shortcuts. Creating art is fundamentally hard work. Embrace the struggle. Flow states come after painful effort, not before.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The work**: Creating art is hard work. There are no shortcuts, no easy paths, no hacks.
- **Flow follows effort**: Flow states come AFTER painful effort, not before. You must earn the flow.
- **Embrace the struggle**: The difficulty IS the point. The struggle produces the quality.

## Key Concepts
- **No shortcuts**: The internet sells shortcuts; real art requires real work
- **Pain precedes pleasure**: The painful part of creation is necessary
- **Consistency over inspiration**: Showing up and working is more important than waiting for inspiration
- **The paradox**: The harder the work, the more pleasurable the flow

## Mental Models
- Use "on work" thinking when you feel like giving up or looking for shortcuts
- Think of the struggle as part of the creative process, not an obstacle to it
- Flow is earned through effort, not given

## Anti-patterns
- **Looking for shortcuts**: Hacks, templates, and shortcuts don't produce quality
- **Waiting for inspiration**: Inspiration comes during work, not before it
- **Avoiding difficulty**: The hard parts are the parts that matter

## Key Takeaways
1. Creating art is fundamentally hard work — there are no shortcuts
2. Flow states come after painful effort, not before
3. Embrace the struggle — it produces the quality
4. Consistency matters more than inspiration — show up and work
5. The difficulty IS the point — it's what separates good from great
