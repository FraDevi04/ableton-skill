# Chapter 9: One Part at a Time

## Core Idea
Instead of sketching all parts broadly, work deeply on ONE element until it's release-ready. This develops focus skill and one great part can inspire others.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Deep work on single element**: Treat one part as the goal itself, not a building block. Make it amazing before adding anything else.
- **Opposite of breadth-first**: Contradicts "Breadth Before Depth" (ch25) — sometimes you need depth before breadth. No single way to work.
- **Micro-completion**: A single part is a tiny version of a complete song — same criteria apply (sonic proficiency, flow over time, interest upon repetition).

## Key Concepts
- **Paralysis of the blank slate**: Thinking about a finished song from nothing is terrifying
- **Focus as skill**: Learning to finish something small teaches you what finishing feels like
- **Catalytic inspiration**: One great part can reveal complementary ideas for other parts
- **Discipline exercise**: If bored with one part but not inspired by another, applying this technique builds discipline

## Mental Models
- Use "one part at a time" when you need to develop focus or when one great part can catalyze others
- Use "breadth before depth" when you need to generate many ideas quickly
- These approaches are opposite but both valid — choose based on your creative block

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating parts as mere building blocks**: Each part deserves to be finished to a high standard
- **Being too strict**: If inspired to work on a different part, follow the inspiration
- **Ignoring unsalvageable parts**: If one part is fundamentally broken, start over rather than forcing it

## Key Takeaways
1. Forget the song for now — begin with just ONE part
2. Treat that one part with the same respect as a finished song
3. Making yourself finish something small teaches you what finishing feels like
4. One great part can inspire complementary ideas for other parts
5. This approach contradicts "breadth before depth" — both are valid, choose based on the situation
